Particles

Particles are a vital part of the Japanese janguage and make the difference between "My dog is eating," and "I am eating my dog,". I'm not quite sure I can give you every meaning of every particle but I can give you the most standard uses of each one. For example, the particle に has maybe 10 or so different uses. I'll only be able to give you example sentences on the simplest uses for now, but I'l try my best to list each particle's main uses as concisely and easy to understand as possible. Yes, all of images are drawn by me.

Indicates the topic of a sentence giving context for what is happening. Note that although in most cases it indicates the topic is the one doing the action, this is not always the case.

e.g. 先生は数学が好きです。
The teacher likes maths.

Indicates the thing that is doing the action and can describe an object. It functions very similar to は.

e.g. 犬が食べています。
The dog is eating.
e.g. 彼は目が小さいです。
His eyes are small.

Indicates a specific time when something happens, movement in actions, direction, intent, and a thing that an object is being affected br or affected from. Similar to the English to, at, or on.

e.g. 私は朝六時に起きました。
I woke up at 6 in the morning.
e.g. 彼はは学校に走っている。
He is running to school.

Indicates the place where an action is occurring or by what means an action happens.

e.g. ジョンさんは大学で英語をおしえます。
John teaches English at university.
e.g. 日本では、箸で食べます。
In Japan, you eat with chopsticks.

Indicates the possession of something or the concept of it. Also can indicate a question and can be combined with か.

e.g. 私の場合は悪いです。
My situation is bad.
e.g. 走るのが好きです。
I like running.
e.g. どうしたの?
What happened?

Indicates a question or can give multiple options. Basically equivalent to the English question mark.

e.g. それは問題ですか。
Is that a problem?
e.g. お風呂にはいるですか。ベッドに幾ですか。
Will you take a bath or go to sleep?

Indicates a complete list of things or multiple things carrying out an action.

e.g. 彼女は学校で数学と理科と日本語を勉強します。
She studies maths, science and Japanese at school.
e.g. 友達と食べたいです。
I want to eat with my friends.

Indicates a desire for attention/response from the listener. Usually only used in speech. Similar to the English "You know?" or "Isn't it?"

e.g. それはいいですね!
That's good isn't it!

Has the same purpose as に when used as a method to convey direction.

e.g. 日本へようこそ!
Welcome to Japan!

Indicates a different place or object having acting out the same verb or having the same verb acted on it. It is the equivalent of 'also', 'as well', and 'too' from English. It can replace を, は, and が.

e.g. 私もそのところに行きました。
I also went to that place.

Indicates an incomplete. The same as と but shows only a few items out of a list.

e.g.音楽や日本語を練習しました。
I practiced music and Japanese.

Japanese equivalent of an exclamation mark.

e.g. なんでだよ？
Why!?

Proceeds the thing that has the action done on it. Distinguishes what is being effected.

e.g.毎日リンゴを一つ食べます。
Everyday I eat 1 apple.
